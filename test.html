Chest pain specific questions:
Grade = D
❌

● Dyspnoea/SOB

  • Shortness of breath is a symptom of heart failure, Cardiovascular disease, heart valve disorders and many other cardiac causes. It is also present in pulmonary causes like COPD, asthma, PE, and pneumonia. You can also get it in anaemia, oesity, anxiety attacks and musculoskeletal issues.

● Orthopnoea

  • This is when you get SOB when lying flat, so patients would normally sleep in an upright position. A good question to ask for would be to ask how many pillows they sleep with at night. Make sure to clarify that it is because of SOB and not because of pain or anything else.

● Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea (PND)

  • This is when you wake up in the middle of the night short of breath/gasping for air.

● Any cough?

  • Good to ask as you can get a cough in conditions such as pneumonia, heart failure, pulmonary embolism and many others. Also important to ask if there's any sputum and it's consistency/colour if sputum is present.

● Family history of heart conditions

  • Important to ask about heart conditions specifically as it can increase risk of Cardiovascular disease. A lot of cardiomyopathies run in families too like Hypertrophiccardiomyopathy (HCM) and dilated cardiomyopathy.

● PMHx of diabetes

  • Having diabetes increases the risk of developing CVD by more than 2-4 times, so is an important risk factor to ask about

● PMHx of hypertension

  • Is an important risk factor

● Any previous heart attacks

  • Important risk factor

● Dizziness/fainting/lightheadedness

  • Can point towards arrhythmias or reduced blood flow to the brain.

● Physical activity

  • Understanding the level of physical activity helps assess lifestyle factors and overall cardiovascular health

● Palpitations/irregular heart beats

  • Can point towards arrhythmias/abnormal heart beats

● Sleep apnoea/snoring

  • Sleep apnoea is linked to an increased risk of CVD

● PMHx of Peripheral Artery Disease

  • Associated with coronary artery disease

● Fever

  • Can be associated with things such as endocarditis, pneumonia

● Changes in weight

  • You can get wt gain from oedema/fluid retention, wt loss in atrial myxoma (tumour in the atria)

● Peripheral oedema

  • Important to ask about any swelling in the ankles as it is a sign of right sided heart failure, which could be one of the differentials in this case.
